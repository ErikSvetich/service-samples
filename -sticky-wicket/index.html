Reading this page

What you’re looking at

Each block below mirrors a real engagement: KPIs, filters, and Chart.js visuals, the same stack we use in our live portfolio samples. Nothing here pulls from your POS; it’s a vision deck for how decisions could feel once data is connected.

Terms we use

  • Baseline (events): typical net sales on a comparable day-of-week without a marquee fixture, used to express “lift.”
  • Index (labor / overview): sales or hours scaled to a common scale so different days compare on one chart (relative, not dollars).
  • Margin index (menu): contribution vs. your bar’s average line; 100 = average for the period.

To go live on your numbers

  • POS category & item sales (CSV export or API)
  • Payroll or schedule hours by role (for labor match)
  • Optional: inventory / pour data for stock health

Tip: use the nav to jump sections. Hover any chart for exact values, same behavior we ship for operators.

02 · Event-linked performance

What actually fills the room

Tie POS history to match nights, tournament windows, and in-house nights like Drag Bingo or Trivia so marketing, staffing, and purchasing move with the calendar, not guesswork.

Revenue lift vs. baseline (last 90 days)

Lift = how much net sales exceeded baseline for that event type, expressed as a rough %. Includes programmed nights (e.g. Drag Bingo, Trivia Night) alongside sports. Bars in cooler tones are still positive but closer to an ordinary night. “No major fixture” anchors near 0% by design.

03 · Labor vs. demand

Right-size every shift

Stack scheduled labor against net sales by hour, then turn gaps into concrete cut/add cues and a modeled dollar bleed when labor runs hotter than demand.

Hourly sales vs. labor (same index)

Green = sales index; amber = scheduled labor hours index. Where amber sits above green, you are paying for capacity you are not selling through (validated against wage rules and minimum coverage in a live build).

    05 · Inventory health

    Cash on the shelf

    Days of cover and slow movers, especially imports and rotating taps, so capital is not trapped before the next big fixture.

    Days of cover with run-out and slow-stock bands

    Days of cover = on-hand ÷ average daily usage (demo). Amber line (~5d): at or below means reorder or you risk a stockout. Violet line (~18d): at or above means cash and cooler space sitting too long (promo, reduce next order, or rotate the tap).

    07 · Automated weekly briefing

    Plain-English “what changed”

    After dashboards go live, layer a weekly narrative (drivers, anomalies, what to watch next) so leadership spends minutes, not hours, in the numbers.

    Sample briefing excerpt

    Headline: Labor % rose on slower Tue–Wed evenings while weekend brunch food margin improved week over week.

    • Driver: +2.4 hrs average on closing shifts vs. prior month with flat midweek sales.
    • Bright spot: Pasty & pub plate mix up 8% during Sat–Sun breakfast opens.
    • Watch next: IPL window: compare staffing to last season’s comparable fixtures.
